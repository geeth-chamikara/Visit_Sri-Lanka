About Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka, officially the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka, is an island country in South Asia. Known for its breathtaking natural beauty, it is often called the **"Pearl of the Indian Ocean."**

The country boasts a rich cultural heritage, with over 2,500 years of recorded history. From ancient ruins and magnificent temples to pristine beaches and lush, rolling tea plantations, Sri Lanka offers a diverse range of experiences for every traveler.

  • Capital: **Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte**, Colombo (Commercial)
  • Official Languages: **Sinhala**, **Tamil**
  • Famous For: **Tea, Gemstones, Wildlife, Ancient Heritage**

Unforgettable Destinations

Historical and cultural Sites



Sigiriya

Sigiriya, also known as the *Lion Rock*, is an ancient rock fortress located in the heart of Sri Lanka. Rising nearly 200 meters above the surrounding plains, it is famous for its stunning frescoes, the Mirror Wall, landscaped water gardens, and the massive lion-shaped gateway that once stood at the entrance. Built in the 5th century by King Kashyapa, Sigiriya is considered one of the best-preserved examples of ancient urban planning and is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is widely admired for its history, engineering, and breathtaking views.

Galle Duch Fort

Galle Dutch Fort, located in the coastal city of Galle in Sri Lanka, is a historic fortress built by the Portuguese in the 16th century and later strengthened by the Dutch in the 17th century. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the fort is known for its well-preserved colonial architecture, cobblestone streets, ancient ramparts, and beautiful ocean views. Today, it stands as a vibrant cultural landmark filled with cafes, museums, shops, and historic buildings, showcasing a unique blend of European and Sri Lankan heritage.

Kandy

Kandy, located in the central hills of Sri Lanka, is a historic city known for its cultural and religious significance. It is home to the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic, one of Buddhism’s most revered sites. Surrounded by lush forests and misty mountains, Kandy features the scenic Kandy Lake, traditional arts, and vibrant festivals such as the Esala Perahera. As a UNESCO World Heritage City, Kandy beautifully blends natural beauty with rich cultural heritage.

See more details

Dambulla Cave Temple

Dambulla Cave Temple, also known as the Golden Temple of Dambulla, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the largest, best-preserved cave temple complex in Sri Lanka. Situated on a massive rock, it contains five main caves filled with stunning Buddha statues, ancient murals, and religious paintings that date back over 2,000 years. The temple offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and stands as a remarkable symbol of Sri Lanka’s Buddhist heritage and artistic history.

Anuradhapura

Anuradhapura is one of Sri Lanka’s most ancient and sacred cities, renowned for its well-preserved ruins of early Sri Lankan civilization. As a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it is home to majestic stupas, ancient monasteries, royal palaces, and the revered Sri Maha Bodhi Tree, believed to be the oldest living tree planted by human hands. Once the powerful capital of Sri Lanka for over a thousand years, Anuradhapura remains a spiritual and historical center that showcases the island’s rich Buddhist heritage.

Polonnaruwa

Polonnaruwa is an ancient royal city in Sri Lanka and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its impressive stone temples, statues, palaces, and irrigation systems. It served as the island’s capital in the 11th–13th centuries and showcases the architectural and engineering brilliance of the era. Landmarks like the Gal Vihara, with its beautifully carved Buddha statues, and the Parakrama Samudraya reservoir highlight Polonnaruwa’s historical and cultural importance

Sri Dalada Maligawa

Sri Dalada Maligawa: Located in Kandy, Sri Lanka, the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic is one of the most sacred Buddhist sites in the world. It houses a relic of Lord Buddha’s tooth and is a symbol of Sri Lankan heritage, culture, and religion. The temple complex features stunning Kandyan architecture, golden roofs, and a tranquil atmosphere, attracting thousands of pilgrims and tourists annually.

Mihinthale

Mihinthales a sacred pilgrimage site in Sri Lanka, believed to be the place where Arahat Mahinda met King Devanampiya Tissa and introduced Buddhism to the island. The site features ancient stairways, stupas, and monasteries. Its peaceful atmosphere and historical significance make it one of Sri Lanka’s most revered religious.

Sri Pada / Adam's Peak

Sri Pada / Adam's Peakis a sacred mountain in Sri Lanka known for the holy footprint at its summit, worshipped by multiple religions. Thousands of pilgrims climb the long staircase each year, especially during the season, to witness the breathtaking sunrise and the famous “shadow of the peak” phenomenon.

Yapahuwa

Yapahuwa was a medieval fortress and royal palace built on a steep rock in north-western Sri Lanka. It once served as the kingdom’s capital and protected the Sacred Tooth Relic. The grand stone staircase, intricate carvings, and impressive defensive structures highlight its historical and architectural importance.

Avukana Buddha Statue

Avukana Buddha Statue is a magnificent 12-meter stone sculpture built during the 5th century. Carved from a single granite rock, it is celebrated for its perfect proportions and detailed craftsmanship. The statue depicts the Buddha in the Abhaya Mudra, symbolizing protection and peace, attracting pilgrims and visitors year-round.

Independence Square

Independence Square in Colombo marks Sri Lanka’s freedom from British rule in 1948. Its memorial hall, inspired by ancient Kandyan architecture, is a national symbol of pride. The peaceful surroundings, landscaped gardens, and jogging paths make it a popular spot for recreation, reflection, and cultural events in the city.

Jaffna

Jaffna, located in northern Sri Lanka, is a vibrant region rich in Tamil culture, history, and tradition. Known for its friendly communities, unique cuisine, and historic sites, Jaffna blends colonial influences with deep-rooted heritage. Key attractions include Jaffna Fort, Nallur Temple, and beautiful northern beaches.

See more details

Nallur Kandaswamy Kovil

Nallur Kandaswamy Kovil is one of Sri Lanka’s most important Hindu temples, dedicated to Lord Murugan. Known for its golden gopuram and vibrant atmosphere, the temple hosts daily rituals and the grand Nallur Festival. Visitors admire its spiritual energy, traditional music, and rich cultural heritage.

Sri Maha bodhi

Sri Maha bodhi in Anuradhapura is the world’s oldest historically documented tree, grown from a branch of the sacred Bodhi tree under which the Buddha attained enlightenment. It is a deeply revered pilgrimage site, surrounded by shrines, offerings, and centuries of Buddhist tradition and devotion.

katharagama

katharagamal is a major pilgrimage town in southern Sri Lanka, sacred to Buddhists, Hindus, and indigenous communities. Devotees visit the Ruhunu Kataragama Maha Devalaya to seek blessings from God Kataragama. The town becomes especially vibrant during the annual festival, featuring rituals, processions, and traditional performances.

Koneshvaram temple

Koneshvaram temple in Trincomalee is a historic Hindu temple perched on a cliff overlooking the Indian Ocean. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, it is known for its breathtaking views, ancient carvings, and deep cultural significance. The temple draws pilgrims and visitors who admire its spiritual atmosphere and scenic beauty.

Kuragala

Kuragala is an ancient archaeological and religious site located in the Sabaragamuwa Province of Sri Lanka. Known for its caves and rock formations, it holds historical significance for Buddhist meditation practices. The site also offers panoramic mountain views, making it a peaceful destination for both spiritual seekers and nature lovers.

Nature and wildlife



Yala National Park

Yala National Park, located in southeastern Sri Lanka, is the country’s most famous wildlife sanctuary. It is renowned for its diverse ecosystems, including forests, wetlands, and lagoons, which are home to elephants, leopards, crocodiles, and hundreds of bird species. Yala offers excellent safari experiences, allowing visitors to observe wildlife in their natural habitat while enjoying the park’s scenic landscapes.

See more details

Mirissa

Mirissa is a popular coastal town in southern Sri Lanka, famous for its beautiful golden beaches, turquoise waters, and relaxing tropical atmosphere. It is one of the best places in the world for whale and dolphin watching, especially blue whales. Mirissa also offers great spots for surfing, snorkeling, and enjoying vibrant beachside cafés. With its stunning sunsets and laid-back vibe, Mirissa is a favorite destination for travelers seeking both adventure and relaxation.

See more details

Ella

Ella is a scenic hill town in Sri Lanka known for its cool climate, lush greenery, and breathtaking mountain views. Surrounded by tea plantations and misty valleys, it offers popular attractions like Ella Rock, Little Adam’s Peak, and the famous Nine Arch Bridge. With its relaxed atmosphere, hiking trails, and natural beauty, Ella is a favorite destination for travelers seeking tranquility and outdoor adventure.

See more details

Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya, often called “Little England”, is a charming hill town in Sri Lanka known for its cool climate, misty mountains, and lush tea plantations. Built during the British colonial era, it features old English-style buildings, beautiful gardens, and scenic landscapes. Popular attractions include Gregory Lake, Horton Plains, and numerous tea factories. With its fresh climate and postcard-like scenery, Nuwara Eliya is one of the most picturesque destinations in the country.

See more details

Udawalawe National Park

Udawalawe National Park, located in southern Sri Lanka, is one of the best places in the country to see wild elephants in their natural habitat. The park features grasslands, forests, and the Udawalawe Reservoir, creating a rich environment for wildlife. It is home to hundreds of elephants, water buffalo, deer, crocodiles, and many bird species. Known for its excellent safari experiences, Udawalawe offers visitors a close and memorable look at Sri Lanka’s wildlife and natural beauty.

See more details

Arugambay

Arugambay is a famous coastal town in eastern Sri Lanka, renowned for its world-class surfing beaches and relaxed tropical vibe. The area attracts surfers from around the globe, especially during the peak season, and is surrounded by lagoons, palms, and wildlife. Besides surfing, visitors can enjoy beachside cafés, nature walks, and stunning sunrises, making Arugambay a perfect destination for both adventure and relaxation.

Galle face

Galle face, is a popular oceanfront promenade in Colombo, Sri Lanka. Stretching along the Indian Ocean, it is ideal for evening walks, kite flying, and enjoying sunsets. Surrounded by hotels, restaurants, and open spaces, it attracts locals and tourists alike, offering a vibrant atmosphere and a refreshing sea breeze experience.

Ravana Ella Falls

Ravana Ella Falls, is a majestic waterfall located in Ella, Sri Lanka. Surrounded by lush greenery and misty hills, it is about 25 meters high. The waterfall is linked to the legendary King Ravana of Sri Lankan folklore and is a popular spot for trekking, sightseeing, and photography for nature enthusiasts.

Gregory Lake

Gregory Lake, is a scenic artificial lake in Nuwara Eliya, Sri Lanka. Surrounded by lush hills and parks, it offers boating, cycling, and horse riding activities. Its calm waters and beautiful scenery make it a popular destination for tourists seeking relaxation, leisure, and picturesque views in the cool climate of the “Little England” of Sri Lanka.

Boburu Ella

Boburu Ella, is a scenic waterfall nestled in the lush forests of Ratnapura, Sri Lanka. Surrounded by greenery and mist, it offers a serene and tranquil environment. Visitors enjoy short treks, photography, and nature walks here, making it a hidden gem for travelers seeking peace and the beauty of Sri Lanka’s waterfalls.

Horton plains national park

Horton plains national park, is a protected area in central Sri Lanka, renowned for its unique biodiversity and montane grasslands. Visitors can enjoy scenic hikes, misty landscapes, and the famous World’s End cliff drop. It is home to endemic wildlife, cloud forests, and beautiful trails, offering an unforgettable experience for nature and adventure enthusiasts.

See more details

Dunhinda waterfall

Dunhinda waterfall, one of Sri Lanka’s tallest and most picturesque waterfalls, located near Badulla. Its misty cascade plunges over 60 meters into a lush valley, creating a serene atmosphere. Accessible via a short trek, it attracts nature lovers, hikers, and photographers who seek the breathtaking beauty and peaceful surroundings of this natural wonder.
